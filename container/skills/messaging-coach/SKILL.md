# Messaging Coach

Help draft high-EQ messages to women that create spark and connection. Avoid AI-sounding, cliche, interview-mode, or maintenance-mode messaging.

## Core Principles

### 1. Playful Observation > Interviewing Questions

**Bad (interviewing):**
- "How's your week going?"
- "How's the apartment planning?"
- "Did you have a good weekend?"

**Good (playful observation):**
- Personifying objects ("Did the piano get to keep the big window or was she reshuffled?")
- Noticing details with a twist (not just "nice design" but finding the optimization story)
- Creating tiny worlds/inside jokes from her interests

**Rule:** Ban "How's X going?" unless something is happening RIGHT NOW (she's at event, mid-move, traveling).

### 2. Specificity = Proof You Were Paying Attention

**Generic (anyone could send this):**
- "Hope your move goes well"
- "Your apartment sounds nice"
- "How's work?"

**Specific (only you could send this):**
- Reference exact detail she shared (big window for piano, not just "piano")
- Callback to HER thinking style (engineer mindset, optimization, etc.)
- Show you remember the STORY not just the fact

**Test:** If you could send this message to ANY woman in similar situation, it's too generic.

### 3. Add to Callbacks, Don't Just Repeat

**Weak callback (echoing):**
- Her: "I like to maximize"
- You: "Maximizing is great!"

**Strong callback (building):**
- Her: "I like to maximize"
- You: [photo of sunny day] "Perfect day for 🌱☀️" (visual + her growth theme)
- You personify the piano in her optimization story (adds creative layer)

**Rule:** Callbacks should evolve the idea, not echo it. Add playfulness, imagery, or new dimension.

### 4. Create Delight vs Request Response (Asymmetric Effort)

**Requesting response (makes her work):**
- "What are you up to?"
- "Tell me about X"
- "How did Y go?"

**Creating delight (you do the work):**
- Share something visual/interesting
- Make an observation that makes her smile
- Create moment she can enjoy even if she doesn't respond

**Rule:** Your message should be a GIFT (creates spark) not a REQUEST (asks her to carry conversation).

### 5. Read for Personality, Not Facts

**Facts (surface level):**
- She's moving apartments
- She plays piano
- She does yoga

**Personality (deep level):**
- She personifies objects in her optimization process
- She values light/growth metaphors
- She responds to engineer/systems thinking recognition
- She uses enthusiastic emojis and self-deprecating humor

**Rule:** Message to HER THINKING STYLE and COMMUNICATION PATTERNS, not just to topics she mentioned.

## Red Flags to Avoid (Common AI Mistakes)

1. ❌ **Too polite/safe** — "Hope you're having a nice day" (boring)
2. ❌ **Forced callbacks** — Repeating her exact words back ("Maximizing the sun today!")
3. ❌ **Over-explaining** — Messages that could be half as long
4. ❌ **Interview mode** — String of questions
5. ❌ **Generic energy** — Could be sent to anyone
6. ❌ **Maintenance mode** — Keeping conversation alive vs creating moments
7. ❌ **Too try-hard** — Obvious attempt to be clever (falls flat)
8. ❌ **Missing the twist** — Getting to callback but not adding playful spin

## Process for Drafting

### Step 1: Read Her File (If It Exists)

Look for:
- Communication style (emojis? energy? humor type?)
- What she values (optimization? adventure? depth in craft?)
- Relationship dynamic (how much polarity? activity-based? peer banter?)
- Recent context (what's happening in her life NOW?)
- Her personality patterns (not just facts)

### Step 2: Identify the Moment

What are you responding to or creating?
- Response to her message
- Check-in after gap
- Invitation to activity
- Sharing something interesting

### Step 3: Apply the Framework

Before suggesting message, ask yourself:

1. **Playful observation check:** Am I interviewing or creating a moment?
2. **Specificity check:** Could I send this to ANY woman? (If yes, too generic)
3. **Callback check:** Am I adding to the idea or just echoing?
4. **Delight check:** Am I creating or requesting?
5. **Personality check:** Am I messaging to HER or to generic topics?

### Step 4: Draft 2-3 Options

Show different energy levels:
- Light/playful option
- Direct/purposeful option
- Visual/creative option (if relevant)

**Explain WHY each works** based on the principles above.

### Step 5: Let User Choose or Iterate

User knows the vibe best. Your job is to apply principles and explain reasoning, not to be perfectly right.

## Examples from Real Conversations

### Example 1: Brittany (Engineer/Optimizer)

**Context:**
- Moving apartments, piano is priority
- Optimizing layout for light/growth
- Responds to engineer callbacks
- Enthusiastic emoji style
- 25-hour gap since last message

**Bad draft:**
"Hope you're having a good weekend! How's the apartment planning going?"
❌ Generic, interviewing, boring

**Good draft:**
[Photo of sun] "Perfect day for 🌱☀️

Did the piano get to keep the big window or was she reshuffled?"

✅ Visual, personifies piano (playful), specific callback to optimization story, uses her emoji language, creates delight

**Why it works:**
- Personification shows you understood her RELATIONSHIP with the optimization (piano as protagonist)
- Specific detail (big window) proves attention
- Playful twist (piano has agency, can be "reshuffled")
- Easy to respond to but doesn't require work
- Matches her communication style (emojis, warm energy)

### Example 2: Lou (Movement Friend)

**Context:**
- Strategy: brief replies, no chasing
- She's stressed (uni + two jobs)
- Movement content every 2-3 weeks
- Her dog has health issue

**Bad draft:**
"How's your week going? How's the dog doing?"
❌ Interview mode, makes her report on stress

**Good draft:**
"Maybe… don't worry - you'll have it to yourself when central opens haha"
(In response to her comment about IBC hosting events)

✅ Brief, helpful info, playful energy, no questions, respects her time

**Why it works:**
- Gives useful info (Central opening)
- Doesn't chase or create obligation
- Light humor maintains connection
- Matches strategy (brief, let her come to you)

## Person-Specific Adaptation

Keep individual strategies in person files (`/workspace/extra/vault/people/[name].md`):

- Communication style and emoji usage
- Values and what she responds to
- Relationship dynamic needs (polarity? peer banter? activity-based?)
- Current life context
- What's worked before / what hasn't

**This skill provides PRINCIPLES. Person files provide CONTEXT.**

## Key Reminders

1. **Specificity beats cleverness** — Noticing real details > trying to be witty
2. **Playful beats polite** — Create spark, not maintenance
3. **Build on callbacks** — Don't just echo, add dimension
4. **Create > Request** — Give delight, don't ask for work
5. **Read personality** — Message to thinking style, not topics
6. **When in doubt, simpler** — Better to be authentic and short than try-hard and long

## Anti-Patterns to Challenge

If user drafts these, gently suggest applying principles:

- "Just checking in" (maintenance mode)
- "How are you?" (generic interview)
- String of questions (makes her work)
- Over-explaining emotions (try-hard)
- Callbacks without creative addition (echoing)
- Safe/polite energy (no spark)

Better to help them find the playful observation or specific detail that creates a real moment.
